# About Norris Cornell

I am a cybersecurity professional whose work spans identity and access management, cyber defense operations, and research aligned to critical infrastructure security.

My professional interests center on how trust, visibility, and system dependencies create both defensive opportunity and attack surface across modern environments.

## Professional focus

I work across cybersecurity domains including IAM, blue-team operations, and infrastructure security, with a particular emphasis on the systems organizations rely on every day.

## Research direction

My writing and analysis focus on:
- ICS / SCADA / OT security
- satellite and timing dependency risk
- signal trust and verification
- cyber hygiene and asset visibility
- defensive systems thinking
- critical infrastructure resilience

## Why this site exists

Cornell Security serves as a professional portfolio, research archive, and writing platform. It is designed to make technical work visible in a form that is useful to recruiters, practitioners, conference organizers, and peers in the cybersecurity community.

## Research disclaimer

All analysis and research presented on this site are based solely on publicly available information and independent study. No confidential, proprietary, or classified information is used or referenced.
