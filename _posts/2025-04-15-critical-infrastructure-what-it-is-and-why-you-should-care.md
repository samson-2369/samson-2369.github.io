Critical infrastructure refers to the systems and assets essential to the functioning of society and the economy—things like power grids, water treatment facilities, transportation networks, and communications. A successful attack on these systems can have cascading effects far beyond a single organization.

In this post, we explore:

- The key sectors designated as critical infrastructure.
- How dependencies between sectors (e.g., power and communications) amplify risk.
- Why adversaries target critical infrastructure and what defenders can do to improve resilience.
